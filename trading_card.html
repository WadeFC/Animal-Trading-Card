White Tiger

The white tiger or bleached tiger is a leucistic pigmentation variant of the Mainland tiger. It has the typical black stripes of a tiger, but carries a white or near-white coat.

Scientific Name: Panthera tigris tigris

Length : 2.7 - 3.1m

Lifespan: 10 to 20 years

Habitat: Tropical Jungles, Mangrove Swamps and Grasslands

The white Bengal tigers are distinctive due to the color of their fur. The white fur caused by a lack of the pigment pheomelanin, which is found in Bengal tigers with orange color fur. When compared to Bengal tigers,

the white Bengal tigers tend to grow faster and heavier than the orange Bengal tiger.
